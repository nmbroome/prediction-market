# Prophet Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: July 11, 2026**

---

## 1. Introduction

Prophet is a prediction market platform operated by Cassandra Laboratories Foundation, Inc. ("Cassandra," "we," "us," or "our"), a Wyoming nonprofit corporation and tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

Prophet exists to produce research. Forecasting activity on the platform generates data used to study how groups aggregate information, how forecasters calibrate, and how prediction markets succeed and fail. That research is conducted by Cassandra and by academic researchers at other institutions, and it is intended for publication.

This means something you should understand before you sign up: **your forecasting activity on Prophet is research data.** This Policy explains what we collect, how it is used, what becomes public, what is shared with researchers, and what rights you have.

By using Prophet, you agree to this Policy. If you do not agree, do not use Prophet.

---

## 2. Scope

This Policy applies to personal information processed in connection with the Prophet platform and any related services. It does not apply to third-party websites or services we do not control.

## 3. Information We Collect

### 3.1 Information You Provide

- **Account details.** Email address, username, and password. If you sign in with Google, we receive your email address and basic profile information from Google.
- **Compensation information.** Your PayPal email address, or, for legacy accounts, an Amazon Mechanical Turk Worker ID. We use this solely to pay you for your participation in research. See Section 4.3.
- **Optional research information.** If you choose to provide it, we may collect optional information used as research covariates, including self-reported cognitive assessment scores and links to assessment results. Providing this is entirely optional and is never a condition of using Prophet or of receiving payouts.
- **Content.** Market questions you create, comments, descriptions, and any other content you submit.
- **Communications.** Support requests, survey responses, and correspondence with us.

### 3.2 Forecasting Activity

Every trade you make is recorded. For each trade we store: the market, the outcome selected, the number of shares, the market probability at the time of the trade, the trade value, whether it was a buy or a sell, and a timestamp. We also record your balance, your realized and unrealized returns, and your positions.

**This is the core research dataset.** See Section 5.

### 3.3 Information We Collect Automatically

- **Usage and log data.** Pages visited, features used, session timing, and interactions with the platform.
- **Device and connection data.** Browser type, operating system, device identifiers, and error and crash reports.
- **Cookies and local storage.** Used to keep you signed in, remember preferences, and measure platform performance.

We use PostHog, a product analytics tool, to collect usage and error data so we can find and fix problems with the platform. PostHog is configured not to capture IP addresses. See Section 10.

### 3.4 Compensation

Trading on Prophet uses an internal platform currency. **You cannot buy it and you cannot cash it out.** You never deposit money and you cannot lose money.

Separately, we compensate you for participating in research. Compensation may include a participation component and a performance-contingent bonus, and it is paid through PayPal (previously Amazon Mechanical Turk). This is payment for research participation, not a redemption of platform currency.

We do not collect or store your PayPal password or any financial account credentials. We store only the PayPal email address you give us so we can pay you. PayPal's handling of your information is governed by PayPal's own privacy policy.

Compensation is taxable income. We may be required to collect a Form W-9 and issue a Form 1099 where annual thresholds are met, and we retain those records as long as tax law requires.

## 4. How We Use Information

We use your information to:

1. **Operate Prophet.** Create and maintain your account, display markets, execute trades, calculate positions, and settle markets on resolution.
2. **Conduct and enable research.** Analyze forecasting behavior, market accuracy, and calibration, and make data available to academic researchers. This is a primary purpose of the platform, not an incidental one. See Section 5.
3. **Compensate you.** Pay you for your research participation, and meet the tax reporting obligations that come with it.
4. **Protect market integrity.** Detect and investigate manipulation, coordinated trading, multi-accounting, and other conduct that corrupts market prices or research data.
5. **Improve the platform.** Diagnose errors, understand feature usage, and fix problems.
6. **Communicate with you.** Send transactional notices such as market resolutions and payout confirmations, and, if you opt in, other updates.
7. **Comply with law.** Respond to lawful requests, meet tax reporting obligations, and enforce our Terms.

## 5. Research Use of Your Data

This section is the most important one in this Policy. Read it.

### 5.1 The Public Research Archive

**When a market resolves, the individual forecast records for that market become part of Prophet's permanent public research archive.**

The archive includes, for each trade on the resolved market: your Prophet username, the outcome selected, the trade size, the market probability at the time of the trade, the trade direction, and the timestamp. It also includes any comments or reasoning you posted on that market.

The archive does **not** include your email address, your PayPal address, your legal name, or any optional research covariates you provided.

**Your live positions are not public.** Nothing about your activity on an open market is disclosed until that market resolves. This protects market integrity and preserves the independence of forecasts.

The archive is permanent. It is published so that researchers, journalists, and the public can independently verify Prophet's forecasting record. Deleting your account does not remove your contributions to the archive of already-resolved markets, though we will disassociate your username from them on request where technically feasible.

### 5.2 Coded Data Shared with External Researchers

Some research questions cannot be answered from the public archive alone. Studies of trader typologies, learning over time, or manipulation detection require linking a single participant's activity across many markets and long time periods.

Using Prophet means participating in this research, and sharing of your coded data is part of that participation (see Section 5.3). For these studies, we may share a **coded** dataset with academic and other researchers at other institutions. In a coded dataset:

- Your username, email address, PayPal address, and legal name are **removed**.
- Your account is replaced with a **research identifier** that is not derivable from your account.
- Your trading history remains linked under that research identifier.

**We want to be direct with you about what this does and does not mean.** Coded data is not anonymous. A dataset that preserves your trading history across markets can, in principle, be linked back to you by someone with enough outside information. We do not claim otherwise, and we do not ask you to rely on a promise that it cannot happen.

Instead, we control it by agreement. Before any coded dataset leaves Cassandra, the receiving institution must sign a Data Use Agreement that:

- prohibits any attempt to re-identify participants;
- prohibits redistribution of the data to anyone outside the approved research team;
- limits use to the specific research protocol described in the agreement;
- requires specified security controls; and
- requires the data to be destroyed or returned at the end of the study.

Where a receiving institution's use of the data requires ethical or institutional review, such as review by an Institutional Review Board, that institution is responsible for obtaining it before using the data.

### 5.3 Participation and Your Data

Prophet is a research platform, and using it means participating in the research it produces. The public archive under Section 5.1 and the coded data sharing under Section 5.2 are **part of participating in Prophet, not separate optional choices.** You cannot use Prophet while excluding your data from this research.

If you do not want your data used this way, do not use Prophet.

You may stop the future use of your data at any time by closing your account. Doing so stops your data from being included in datasets shared after that point. It cannot retract datasets already delivered to researchers, unpublish research already published, or remove your contributions from the public archive under Section 5.1.

### 5.4 Aggregate and Published Results

Research findings, statistics, calibration curves, and other aggregate results derived from Prophet data may be published without restriction.

## 6. How We Share Information

We share information:

- **Publicly**, through the research archive described in Section 5.1, and through your username, profile, and leaderboard standing.
- **With academic researchers**, as described in Section 5.2, under a Data Use Agreement.
- **With service providers** who host our infrastructure, process payouts, send email, and provide analytics and error monitoring. These providers are bound by contract to process data only on our instructions. Our current providers include Supabase (database and authentication), Vercel (hosting), PayPal (payouts), and PostHog (product analytics).
- **For legal reasons**, to comply with law, respond to lawful process, meet tax reporting obligations, or protect the rights and safety of Prophet users.
- **In a transfer of operations**, if Prophet is transferred to another nonprofit or research institution. We will notify you.

**We do not sell your personal information.** We are a nonprofit and we do not run advertising.

## 7. Your Rights and Choices

You may:

- **Access** a copy of the data we hold about you.
- **Correct** inaccurate information.
- **Delete** your account. See Section 5.1 for what deletion does and does not reach, and Section 5.3 for how closing your account stops future use of your data.
- **Withdraw consent** for any processing that is based on your consent, such as the optional research covariates described in Section 3.1.
- **Opt out of non-essential email** at any time.
- **Object or restrict** processing in certain circumstances.

To exercise any of these, contact us or use your account settings.

## 8. Legal Bases (EEA and UK)

If you are in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we process your personal data on the following bases:

- **Contract** (Art. 6(1)(b)) to operate your account, execute trades, pay you, and provide the research participation that is the core purpose of the platform, including the public archive and coded data sharing described in Section 5.
- **Public interest and scientific research** (Art. 6(1)(e) and Art. 89) for the research described in Section 5, with appropriate safeguards including data minimization and coding.
- **Consent** (Art. 6(1)(a)) for the optional research covariates described in Section 3.1. You may withdraw this consent at any time.
- **Legitimate interests** (Art. 6(1)(f)) to protect market integrity, prevent fraud and manipulation, secure the platform, and diagnose errors.
- **Legal obligation** (Art. 6(1)(c)) where processing is required by law, including tax reporting.

## 9. Retention

We retain account information for as long as your account is active, and after closure only as long as needed to meet legal, tax, and audit obligations, resolve disputes, and prevent fraud and abuse.

Payout records are retained as long as required by tax law.

**The public research archive under Section 5.1 is permanent.** This is intentional. A forecasting track record that can be selectively deleted is not a track record.

## 10. Analytics and Cookies

We use cookies and local storage to keep you signed in, remember your preferences, and understand how the platform is used.

We use PostHog for product analytics and error monitoring. PostHog is configured to disable IP address capture. We do not use analytics data for advertising, and we do not share it with advertising networks.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling some cookies will limit platform functionality.

## 11. Security

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your information, including encryption in transit, database-level access controls, and restricted access to identified data. No system is completely secure. Use a strong, unique password.
